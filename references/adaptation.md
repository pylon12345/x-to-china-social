# Adaptation rules

## Fidelity layers

Separate three layers in the output:

1. What the original author stated
2. Necessary context or translation notes
3. The editor's interpretation or takeaway

Never blend layer 3 into layer 1. Preserve uncertainty, qualifications, numbers, product names, and links. Translate meaning naturally instead of mirroring English sentence order.

## Xiaohongshu

Lead with the reader benefit or surprising conclusion. Use short paragraphs and concrete subheads. Remove X-specific conversational debris only when it does not change meaning. Keep the body useful without clickbait or fabricated personal experience. End with `来源：作者名（@handle），X 原文：URL` and disclose `翻译整理` or `摘要改写`.

## WeChat

Add enough introduction for readers who did not see the original discussion. Use a clear hierarchy and retain supporting reasoning. Put editor additions under labels such as `编者按` or `我的观察`. End with a source section listing the original author and canonical URL.

## Rights-sensitive content

For a short public post, quote only what is needed and add commentary/context. For long threads and X Articles, prefer a Chinese summary with selective short quotations. Keep original media attribution and do not download or republish media when rights are unclear.
